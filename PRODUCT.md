# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The product is used by platform administrators, agents, and operations staff who work in a backend system for plugin orders, upstream integration, configuration, order lookup, and maintenance. They use it repeatedly during operational workflows, usually with concrete tasks to complete rather than browsing or discovery.

## Product Purpose

This system replaces the older admin frontend while preserving existing backend, permission, route, and menu contracts. It exists to keep plugin and order operations usable in `admin-next`, close core business workflows inside the new Art Design Pro based UI, and make migrated pages feel consistent, compact, and reliable.

## Brand Personality

Restrained, business-first, reliable. The interface should feel mature and operational: clear enough to trust, dense enough for repeated use, and calm enough that the work stays in focus.

## Anti-references

Do not reproduce the old Vben page style or mix incompatible visual systems. Avoid marketing-style dashboards, oversized display headers, empty card heads, fake statistics, decorative summary blocks, demo-like modules, and layouts that make users hunt for the next business step.

## Design Principles

1. Start with the business workflow: pages should follow the order in which users actually complete the task.
2. Preserve contracts first: backend APIs, permissions, menu keys, and route names remain stable unless a task explicitly requires otherwise.
3. Prefer native system components: Art Design Pro and Element Plus patterns should carry tables, forms, drawers, dialogs, and feedback.
4. Keep hierarchy useful, not decorative: cards, sections, and labels should clarify the work rather than add visual noise.
5. Make operational state obvious: loading, errors, empty data, disabled actions, and pricing expectations should be visible near the action they affect.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use a practical WCAG AA baseline: readable text contrast, visible keyboard focus, clear form errors, responsive layouts that do not overflow on mobile, and restrained motion with no decorative choreography. Controls should remain recognizable and operable for repeated admin use.
